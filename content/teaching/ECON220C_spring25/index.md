### General Info 
Econ 220C develops the econometric methods used to analyze panel data, where the same units are observed over several periods, in four parts. The first part studies the static linear panel model, starting from pooled OLS and the random-effects estimator and then turning to the fixed-effects model, its identification and consistency, instrumental variables, and clustered inference with time effects. The second part moves to dynamic panel models, where past outcomes influence current ones, and to modern difference-in-differences designs with staggered adoption. The third part develops the theory of extremum estimation, covering the general consistency theorem and the uniform law of large numbers, which prepares the ground for the nonlinear models that follow. The last part applies this framework to limited and qualitative dependent variables, including binary choice and the probit model, censored and truncated regression, and sample selection. The asymptotic theory of extremum estimators, with the method of moments as a special case, provides the unifying thread throughout.

### Material 
1. <span class="hl-term">Introduction to static panel data</span>: data structure, pooled OLS, and the random-effects model. ([Slides](https://lapobini.github.io/discussion/ECON220C_spring25/ds1.pdf))
2. <span class="hl-term">Fixed-effects model</span>: consistency, identification, instrumental variables, and clustering. ([Slides](https://lapobini.github.io/discussion/ECON220C_spring25/ds2.pdf))
3. <span class="hl-term">Clustering and time fixed effects</span>: clustered inference and adding time effects to the within model. ([Slides](https://lapobini.github.io/discussion/ECON220C_spring25/ds3.pdf))
4. <span class="hl-term">Dynamic panel data and staggered DID</span>: persistence in panels and modern difference-in-differences. ([Slides](https://lapobini.github.io/discussion/ECON220C_spring25/ds4.pdf))
5. <span class="hl-term">Practice test</span>: static and dynamic panel data. ([Slides](https://lapobini.github.io/discussion/ECON220C_spring25/ds5.pdf))
6. <span class="hl-term">M-estimation</span>: the general consistency theorem, the uniform law of large numbers, and exercises on NLS and QMLE. ([Slides](https://lapobini.github.io/discussion/ECON220C_spring25/ds6.pdf))
7. <span class="hl-term">Probit model</span>: linear projection, maximum likelihood, instrumental variables, and the uniform law of large numbers. ([Slides](https://lapobini.github.io/discussion/ECON220C_spring25/ds7.pdf))
8. <span class="hl-term">Practice final</span>: binary outcomes with endogeneity, the control function, and discrete choice. ([Slides](https://lapobini.github.io/discussion/ECON220C_spring25/ds8.pdf))
9. <span class="hl-term">Practice final</span>: truncated and censored (Tobit) regression and sample selection. ([Slides](https://lapobini.github.io/discussion/ECON220C_spring25/ds9.pdf))
